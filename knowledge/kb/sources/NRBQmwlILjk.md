## Concepts covered

- [[concepts/organizational-ai-transformation]] — Shopify's coding agent "River" is public-by-default (no DMs; ~1 in 8 merged PRs); closing the widening "apprenticeship gap" requires making the task, context, interaction, and review visible — and senior people working in public.
- [[concepts/open-brain-systems]] — A static prompt library misses the messy context, revisions, and "no, that's wrong for our customer" corrections; the reusable asset is the surrounding habit, not the prompt.
- [[concepts/ai-security-and-trust]] — Don't make all AI chats public (drives work underground); use "declared spaces + declared rules" and a safe public surface that teaches without exposing PII/HR/legal (compliant even under HIPAA).
- [[concepts/enterprise-ai-adoption]] — Most companies already bought the tools; the real gap is visibility, and the right metrics are learning/reuse (reusable workflows created, adopted, pinned), not token volume.

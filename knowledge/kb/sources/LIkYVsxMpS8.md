# When to Automate, Build, Buy, Hire, or Wait on AI

**Video:** [https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=LIkYVsxMpS8](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=LIkYVsxMpS8) • **ID:** `LIkYVsxMpS8` • **Playlists:** P3

## Concepts covered

- [[concepts/practical-agent-adoption]] — AI investment is a workflow question, not an AI question: the unit of decision is the workflow (the whole operating loop), not the department or role; the model is a tiny part of the loop.
- [[concepts/ai-roi-and-value-proposition]] — Gartner: >40% of agentic AI projects die by 2027 from cost, unclear value, and weak risk controls (not the tech); vendor demos win on the routine case while production traffic is exceptions.
- [[concepts/model-selection-frameworks]] — Five levers (automate / build / buy / hire / wait) chosen per workflow on an investment matrix of company-specificity × market maturity; buy primitives and own the workflow standard.
- [[concepts/enterprise-ai-adoption]] — Piling many workflows into one RFP yields a mediocre tool; whole-vendor workflow solutions need ~80–90% shape overlap with your work, else you do more adjustment than expected.

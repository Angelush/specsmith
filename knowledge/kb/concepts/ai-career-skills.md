# AI Career Skills and Fluency

AI Career Skills and Fluency encompasses the critical capabilities required to thrive in the evolving AI-driven professional landscape. This includes mastering intent specification for directing AI agents, developing comprehensive AI fluency across various frameworks, and strategically navigating the K-shaped job market. Success hinges on adapting to new roles, understanding AI's mechanics, and cultivating durable, high-agency work habits that differentiate individuals from commoditized tasks.

## Why it matters

In an era where AI is rapidly commodifying traditional knowledge work, developing specific AI career skills is paramount for professional relevance and growth. These skills not only ensure job security by focusing on AI-resistant and high-leverage activities but also enable individuals to amplify their impact through effective AI orchestration, critical evaluation, and strategic decision-making. Cultivating AI fluency and high agency becomes the primary driver of market value and career advancement.

## Key insights

-   **Intent specification is paramount** — The ability to precisely specify intent is the core skill for AI power users, enabling autonomous agents to execute complex goals and significantly boosting output. It involves translating fuzzy goals into measurable success criteria, constraints, and defined input/output artifacts. [[sources/hDpjMJw3flk]] (CRR-001) [[sources/4cuT-LKcmWs]] (CRR-003)
-   **Durable work focuses on question-holding** — Durable work involves identifying and holding open the right, often ambiguous, questions (question-holding) and exercising senior judgment that cannot be easily specified or automated, rather than merely answering predefined questions. Over-documenting or over-specifying judgment-based work risks commoditizing it, making it susceptible to AI displacement. [[sources/rYqt6mMlv7o]] (CAR-002) [[sources/rYqt6mMlv7o]] (CAR-003) [[sources/rYqt6mMlv7o]] (FWK-054) [[sources/rYqt6mMlv7o]] (PRN-005)
-   **The AI job market is K-shaped** — The AI job market is bifurcating, with high-demand AI roles offering significant compensation and growth, while undifferentiated knowledge worker roles face compression. Key in-demand skills include specification precision, evaluation, multi-agent decomposition, and failure pattern recognition. [[sources/4cuT-LKcmWs]] (CRR-003) [[sources/HZ9iL_lFYgQ]] (MND-006)
-   **AI fluency is a multi-dimensional skill** — Real AI fluency goes beyond basic tool use, encompassing AI strategy, advanced prompting, workflow integration, critical evaluation, and ethical considerations. Frameworks exist (e.g., 10-level or 5-pillar models) to assess and develop this fluency, moving professionals from basic use to systematization and leadership in AI adoption. [[sources/DdlMoRSojtE]] (CRR-007) [[sources/HDVG8RKYX9s]] (CRR-008) [[sources/BYKUwsQOA8U]] (FWK-023)
-   **Skill trees shift to orchestration** — The unit of technical leverage has shifted from writing code to orchestrating probabilistic AI systems. New dominant roles in the agentic economy include tool-using generalists, pipeline engineers, business dealers, and mature decision-makers. Domain expertise, while foundational, is no longer a differentiator; the ability to orchestrate AI systems is. [[sources/fyHnGHxGuhI]] (CRR-012) [[sources/q6p-_W6_VoM]] (CRR-016) [[sources/XlfumXPPrLY]] (FWK-039)
-   **Market value requires articulation and leverage** — In the AI era, individual knowledge worker value is harder to ascertain as AI commoditizes output. The ability to articulate one's operating system, delegate effectively, and make one's impact visible becomes crucial for market value and compensation, as invisibility leads to a widening salary gap. [[sources/-dJ9WrTG6zQ]] (CRR-020) [[sources/rYqt6mMlv7o]] (PRN-005)
-   **High agency is a key career differentiator** — High agency—the belief that outcomes are within one's control and the willingness to act without explicit permission—is a critical career differentiator, accelerated by AI. It fosters continuous skill development and proactive problem-solving. [[sources/HZ9iL_lFYgQ]] (MND-006)
-   **Manage AI like a capable-but-inexperienced employee** — Successful AI adoption treats AI like a capable but inexperienced employee requiring management skills such as task decomposition, rigorous quality assessment, iterative refinement, and judgment regarding its "jagged capability frontier." [[sources/EZ4EjJ0iDDQ]] (MND-005)
-   **Critical thinking demands adversarial checks** — Guard against "LLM psychosis" by proactively challenging AI-backed conclusions with adversarial prompts and consulting domain experts. AI agreement is not a substitute for expert validation, especially in unfamiliar domains. [[sources/AzOJ9QLgfIk]] (MND-004)
-   **AI can power career accountability** — AI can be leveraged to create structured quarterly career accountability systems, acting as an "AI board of directors" to provide rigorous, unbiased feedback and identify areas for growth, which is particularly valuable in the absence of human mentors. [[sources/BaC5FEN2e4Y]] (CRR-006)
- **A personal truth layer for the prove-it economy** — In the interpretation economy, individuals need a personal "truth layer" that demonstrably proves their AI skills (vetting and proof matter to land reliable AI-economy roles), not an AI-washed LinkedIn — the same agent-legibility problem products face [[sources/725QE_LNXT4]].

## Prompt commands

### Audit the frame — `CAR-002`
```
I'm going to describe a situation where I've been asked to [PRODUCE DELIVERABLE / ANSWER QUESTION]. Before I execute, help me audit the frame: (1) Is this the right question to be answering, or is there a more fundamental question underneath it? (2) What would a "wrong frame" version of this request look like, and does the current request match it? (3) What is the cost of answering this question well vs. the cost of holding it open for [X more days/weeks]? (4) What would I need to know or see before I'm confident this is the right question? Give me your honest assessment before I proceed.
```

### Craft "partial legibility" narrative — `CAR-003`
```
I'm going to describe a piece of work I do that involves significant judgment and is hard to document fully: [DESCRIBE WORK]. Help me craft a "partial legibility" narrative for it: (1) What outcome can I point to clearly without exposing the mechanism? (2) What 1-2 sentence framing communicates the value of this work at a performance review or in a casual manager conversation? (3) What parts of this work, if I tried to fully specify them, would I likely get wrong or turn into a commodity process? Output a short "credit narrative" I can use and a list of aspects I should NOT over-explain.
```

### Convert fuzzy goal to intent spec — `CRR-001`
```
Convert this fuzzy goal into an executable intent spec for an autonomous agent: [GOAL]. Include: (1) Success criteria — what does done look like, measurable? (2) Constraints — what must not change, what is out of scope? (3) Input artifacts — what does the agent need to start? (4) Output artifacts — what format/location? (5) Edge cases and failure modes to handle explicitly.
```

### Self-assess 7 AI job market skills — `CRR-003`
```
Self-assess against the 7 AI job market skills (score 1-5 each): (1) Specification precision — can I write English that machines take literally with explicit constraints and success criteria? (2) Evaluation/quality judgment — can I detect errors, edge cases, confident-wrong AI output? (3) Multi-agent decomposition — can I size work to harnesses and design planner+sub-agent systems? (4) Failure pattern recognition — do I know the 6 agent failure types? (5) Workflow design (6) Data quality (7) Observability. Identify the 2 lowest scores and create a 30-day improvement plan.
```

### Assess AI fluency level — `CRR-007`
```
Assess my current AI fluency level on a 1-10 scale. Ask me 5 diagnostic questions about how I currently use AI, then place me on the scale with specific evidence from my answers. For my current level, identify: (1) the one mental model I am missing that would move me to the next level, (2) a specific daily practice to build that mental model, (3) a 90-day milestone that would confirm I have reached the next level.
```

### Evaluate 5 AI fluency dimensions — `CRR-008`
```
Evaluate my AI fluency across these 5 dimensions for a [ROLE/CONTEXT]: (1) Strategy — can I identify which AI tasks deliver the most leverage in my work? (2) Prompting — do I adapt my prompting approach across different models and task types? (3) Workflow integration — is AI embedded in my core workflows or bolted on? (4) Critical evaluation — can I reliably identify flawed AI output and articulate why? (5) Ethics — do I understand how to design trust into AI-assisted outputs? For each, rate me 1-5 and suggest one specific improvement action.
```

### Plan AI agent workflows — `CRR-016`
```
Given my domain expertise in [FIELD] and my current AI skill level [DESCRIBE], identify: (1) the three highest-leverage AI agent workflows I could build to amplify my domain work; (2) the "software-shaped" thinking gaps I need to close to direct agents effectively; (3) a 90-day learning plan to move from consumer of AI tools to orchestrator of AI agents.
```

### Assess articulation and leverage — `CRR-020`
```
Assess your articulation and leverage: (1) How visible is your actual operating system to your manager or peers? (2) If you left, how much would your expertise vanish? (3) How many of your work hours could an agent handle if you had a well-configured system? (4) Does your compensation reflect your leverage, or are you underpriced relative to an articulate peer?
```

### TCLD Job Audit — `FWK-054`
```
I'm going to paste a list of items from my last 10 business days (calendar events, emails sent, Slack messages, docs produced, decisions made). For each item, classify it as one of: T (Theater — exists for performance, not examined value), C (Commodity — real work but anyone with a spec could do it), L (On the Line — judgment involved but hard to articulate exactly what), or D (Durable — outcome depended on something that cannot be pre-specified). Use only the item title and any context I provide. Output a table: Item | Tag | One-line reason. At the end, give me the T+C percentage and a 2-sentence summary of where my personal claim on my work is weakest. Here is the list: [PASTE ITEMS]
```

### Reframe obstacle as skill issue — `MND-006`
```
I'm facing [OBSTACLE/CAREER CHALLENGE]. Help me reframe this as a skill issue by: (1) identifying exactly what skills or knowledge would make this fully within my control; (2) listing the fastest 3 ways to acquire those skills using AI; (3) suggesting one concrete action I can take in the next 24 hours to make visible progress regardless of external factors.
```

### Quarterly career interview — `CRR-006`
```
Conduct a quarterly career interview. Your role is a rigorous, neutral evaluator. Interview me by asking one question at a time — do not move on until you have a complete, specific answer. Cover: (1) What did I commit to at the start of this quarter and what specifically happened with each commitment? (2) What decisions did I make and what did I optimize for vs. what did I sacrifice? (3) What did I avoid that I told myself was fine? (4) What patterns do you see in my answers? Do not accept vague answers. Push for specifics. At the end, produce a board report: overall performance assessment, top 3 strengths demonstrated, top 3 weaknesses or avoidance patterns, and 3 concrete action items for next quarter.
```

### Adversarial check for AI conclusions — `MND-004`
```
Before publishing or acting on a conclusion reached with AI on [TOPIC], run an adversarial check: "You previously helped me develop this conclusion: [CONCLUSION]. Now argue against it as strongly as you can. What are the 3 strongest objections from domain experts in [FIELD]? What evidence or reasoning would convince a skeptical expert that this is wrong? What are the most likely ways I am mistaken?" Then consult at least one domain expert who has not seen the AI output.
```

### Evaluate AI for specific task — `MND-005`
```
I need to evaluate whether to use AI for [SPECIFIC TASK]. Help me assess: (1) Is this task inside or outside AI's current capability frontier? What are the specific failure modes if it's outside? (2) How would I decompose this task so AI handles the parts it's good at and I handle the rest? (3) What does "good output" look like, and how will I verify quality before using the result? (4) What is my iterative refinement plan if the first output is wrong? Give me a go/no-go recommendation with reasoning.
```

# AI Economy and Bottlenecks

The AI economy refers to the evolving landscape where artificial intelligence significantly impacts traditional economic principles, creates new resource bottlenecks, and influences strategic decisions for businesses and individuals. It's an idea that recognizes the profound shifts AI introduces to value creation, scarcity, and competitive advantage.

## Why it matters

AI's rapid advancement is fundamentally reshaping markets by creating an abundance of intelligent capabilities, which, in turn, shifts scarcity to downstream factors like physical infrastructure, trust verification, and organizational integration. Understanding these new bottlenecks is crucial for strategic investment, career planning, and maintaining competitive advantage, as ignoring them can lead to significant cost exposures and displacement risks. This new economic reality compels a re-evaluation of traditional business models and individual skill sets.

## Key insights
- **AI mega-IPOs will force index funds into scarcity-inflated buying** — SpaceX, OpenAI, and Anthropic plan IPOs collectively valued at ~$3T while floating only 3-5% of shares, creating artificial scarcity. New NASDAQ rules allow index inclusion after 15 trading days (vs. prior months), weighted by total market cap — compelling index funds to buy at peak prices within weeks. When lock-up expires (3-6 months), insiders holding the other 97% can sell into fund-driven demand. Anyone with a 401k or target-date fund is exposed automatically. [[sources/9N7qXkmntlU]] (TRD-014)
- **AI creates new scarcities** — The abundance of intelligence generated by AI shifts binding constraints downstream to physical infrastructure (compute, energy, skilled labor), trust verification layers for AI-generated content, and organizational change management for effective AI implementation. [[sources/pxuXV3Q6tGY]] (MND-011)
- **Inference economics drive the stack** — The AI industry has entered an "industrial phase" where continuous, latency-bound, and cost-sensitive inference serving, especially for large context windows, dictates infrastructure strategy and multi-vendor chip procurement. [[sources/5Kp-Gj5qXL0]] (TRD-011)
- **Structural compute crisis** — A significant AI compute crisis is unfolding through 2028, marked by fully allocated high-bandwidth memory and hyperscalers locking up supply, meaning enterprises must account for agentic token consumption (10-100B tokens/worker/year) to secure sustained competitive advantage. [[sources/pSgy2P2q790]] (TRD-036)
- **Token economy transforms developer roles** — The unit of work in computing has transitioned to the token, leading to explosive consumption (Jevons' paradox) and requiring developers to specialize in new career tracks: Orchestrator, Systems Builder, or Domain Translator. [[sources/-bQcWs1Z9a0]] (TRD-042)
- **Cloud AI consumer unit economics are broken** — Frontier labs lose money on top-tier consumer subscriptions, with investor capital subsidizing losses, leading to a two-tier AI access system where enterprises receive dedicated capacity while consumer users face metered and throttled access. [[sources/RaAFquzj5B8]] (TRD-049)
- **Human skills for agentic future** — The massive demand demonstrated by agentic systems highlights that the "AI bubble" is actually an "underbuilt" infrastructure challenge, emphasizing the survival and increasing value of four durable human skills: taste, exquisite domain judgment, phenomenal learning speed, and relentless honesty about value migration. [[sources/NCgdpbEvNVA]] (TRD-027)
- **SaaS's "second meter"** — Vendors are adding a second, usage-based meter for delegated agent work on top of per-seat pricing (Salesforce Agentforce bills "agentic work units" — $800M ARR, +169% YoY, 2.4B work units; Microsoft Copilot credits at varying rates; ServiceNow's action fabric); the seat was always a proxy for human value, now metered as delegated work [[sources/adNErrz2aA0]].

### Bottleneck Identification — `MND-011`
```
For my industry [DESCRIBE INDUSTRY] and role [DESCRIBE ROLE], identify: (1) the top three bottlenecks that AI abundance is creating or intensifying; (2) which of these bottlenecks I am positioned to help solve; (3) what specific skill, relationship, or infrastructure investment would give me a compounding advantage as those bottlenecks tighten.
```

### Infrastructure Landscape — `TRD-011`
```
[REFERENCE ONLY — infrastructure landscape, CES 2026] AI entered industrial phase. Inference economics now drive the stack: continuous, latency-bound, cost-sensitive serving at 10M+ token contexts. Nvidia's Vera Rubin = rack-scale AI factory platform. DRAM prices up 300% Q4 2025. Multi-winner chip landscape (like multi-cloud) because demand outpaces any single vendor. Use this when planning infrastructure costs and capacity for agent deployments.
```

### Compute Cost Exposure — `TRD-036`
```
Estimate our organization's AI compute cost exposure: given [NUMBER OF EMPLOYEES], current AI tool usage [DESCRIBE], and planned agentic deployments [DESCRIBE], model three scenarios: (1) human-only usage at 1B tokens/worker/year; (2) hybrid human + agents at 10B tokens/worker/year; (3) aggressive agentic deployment at 100B tokens/worker/year. At a blended rate of $2/million tokens, what is annual inference spend in each scenario, and what procurement actions should we take now?
```

### Developer Career Tracks — `TRD-042`
```
Map my current role against the three token-economy developer tracks: (1) Orchestrator: Do I specify outcomes and manage intelligence budgets, or do I still write the logic myself? What would I need to change to move toward orchestration? (2) Systems builder: Do I build eval frameworks, routing layers, or context management infrastructure? What deep systems expertise could I develop? (3) Domain translator: What is my deepest domain expertise where I know which problems are worth solving and have customer context no generalist AI has? How could I build tools instead of just using them in that domain?
```

### AI Displacement Risk Audit — `TRD-027`
```
Help me audit my current role for AI displacement risk. My role: [TITLE]. My top 5 daily activities: [LIST]. For each activity: (1) Rate how automatable it is today (1-10); (2) Rate how automatable in 18 months given agent trajectory; (3) Identify which human skill (taste, domain judgment, learning speed, honest self-assessment) is my edge if automated; (4) Suggest one concrete action to strengthen that edge. Format as a table.
```
